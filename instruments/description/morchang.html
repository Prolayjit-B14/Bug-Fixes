Morchang

Origins and History:

The Morchang is a traditional Indian musical instrument, commonly used in folk and classical music, particularly in Rajasthan.

Instrument Features:

It is a small metallic instrument, played by striking its flexible metal tongue while altering mouth shape for resonance.

Musical Role:

The Morchang is often used in folk music performances and has a distinct rhythmic and drone-like sound.

Conclusion

The Morchang remains an essential instrument in Indian folk traditions, known for its unique tonal qualities.
