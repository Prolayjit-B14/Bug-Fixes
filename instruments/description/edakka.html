Ekdakka

Origins and History:

The Ekdakka is a traditional percussion instrument from South India, often used in temple music and folk performances.

Instrument Features:

It is a small, hourglass-shaped drum that produces distinct rhythmic patterns and is played with the hands.

Musical Role:

The Ekdakka is commonly used in classical music and devotional settings, providing a dynamic rhythm.

Conclusion

The Ekdakka remains an integral part of Indian percussion traditions.
