Rabab

Origins and History:

The Rabab is a traditional string instrument that has deep roots in Central and South Asian music.

Instrument Features:

It is known for its hollow wooden body and gut strings, which produce a rich and resonant tone.

Musical Role:

The Rabab is widely used in Afghan, Persian, and Indian classical music, known for its soulful melodies.

Conclusion

The Rabab continues to be a revered instrument, played by musicians who appreciate its deep, emotive sound.
