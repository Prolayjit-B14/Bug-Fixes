Chenda

Origins and History:

The Chenda is a traditional percussion instrument from Kerala, India, known for its use in temple rituals, classical music, and folk performances.

Instrument Features:

It is a cylindrical drum with both ends covered with skin, producing a loud and resonant sound when played with sticks.

Musical Role:

The Chenda is prominently used in Kathakali, Theyyam, and temple festivals, adding rhythm and energy to performances.

Conclusion

The Chenda remains a significant instrument in South Indian musical traditions.
