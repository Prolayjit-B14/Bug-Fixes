Santoor

Origins and History:

Ancient Roots: The santoor has a rich history, originating from ancient Persia and later becoming integral to Indian classical music.

Evolution: Originally a folk instrument, Pandit Shivkumar Sharma introduced it to Hindustani classical music.

Instrument Features:

Wooden Construction: Trapezoidal-shaped with a hollow body for resonance.

Strings and Bridges: Features multiple metal strings stretched over wooden bridges.

Played with Mallets: Played using wooden mallets (mezrab) for a unique sound.

Musical Role:

Hindustani Classical Music: Known for its melodic, rich sound.

Folk and Devotional Music: Deeply rooted in Kashmiri folk and Sufi traditions.

Global Influence: Used in fusion, world music, and orchestras.

Key Aspects:

Kashmiri Culture: An essential part of traditional Sufi compositions.

Sound and Expression: Produces a harp-like sound with shimmering quality.

Technical Aspects: Requires precise striking techniques and tonal modulation.
