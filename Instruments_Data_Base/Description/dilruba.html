Dilruba

Origins and History:

The Dilruba is a bowed string instrument originating from India, commonly used in Sikh and Hindustani classical music.

Instrument Features:

It has a long, fretted neck and sympathetic strings, producing a rich and resonant sound.

Musical Role:

The Dilruba is often played in devotional music, classical performances, and folk traditions.

Conclusion

The Dilruba continues to be an integral part of Indian musical heritage.
