Chikara

Origins and History:

The Chikara is a traditional bowed string instrument used in folk music of Rajasthan and North India.

Instrument Features:

It has a wooden body with gut strings and is played with a bow, producing a rich, resonant sound.

Musical Role:

The Chikara is commonly used in folk and devotional music, accompanying traditional Rajasthani songs.

Conclusion

The Chikara continues to be an integral part of Indian folk heritage and musical traditions.
