Sundari

Origins and History:

The Sundari is a traditional Indian wind instrument, closely related to the shehnai but smaller in size and higher in pitch.

Instrument Features:

It is a double-reed instrument, typically made of wood, and is used in folk and classical music performances.

Musical Role:

Sundari is known for its bright, melodious sound and is often played in festive and spiritual gatherings.

Conclusion

The Sundari remains a cherished instrument in Indian folk traditions and continues to enchant listeners with its unique sound.
